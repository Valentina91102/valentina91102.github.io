Summer of Science

Results

The optimal dose of calcium peroxide (CaO₂) was 5 mg/L for 500,000 cells/mL and 10 mg/L for 1,000,000 cells/mL, equating to 1 mg/L per 100,000 cells/mL. Both CaO₂ + PAC and CaO₂ + PAC + kaolinite combinations effectively flocculated and sank *Microcystis*, with kaolinite enhancing sedimentation speed. Smaller volumes (50 mL) showed better efficiency, but recovery occurred within 24 hours in larger volumes (125 mL), likely due to uneven mixing and a larger surface area. The treatments did not reduce microcystin toxin levels, which remained high throughout the study. Future research should optimize component proportions, improve toxin removal (e.g., using biochar), evaluate ecological impacts, and test the treatment on field-collected Microcystis.
